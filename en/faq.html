Compliance & RGIE

RGIE compliance is mandatory in three main situations:

  • Property sale: A passing inspection is required to complete the transaction
  • New installation: Any new installation must be validated by an accredited body
  • Major modification: During substantial works (extension, major renovation, new panel)

The inspection is conducted by an accredited body that issues a compliance certificate valid for 25 years (if no modifications are made).

A non-compliant installation doesn't prevent sale, but has several consequences:

  • Price negotiation: The buyer typically requests a discount
  • Compliance deadline: The buyer gets 18 months to carry out the necessary works
  • Safety risk: A faulty installation presents genuine hazards

Our recommendation: Address compliance before selling. This secures the transaction, adds value to the property, and avoids difficult negotiations.

An RCD that trips frequently indicates a current leakage problem.

Possible causes:

  • Defective appliance: Washing machine, refrigerator, oven... (faulty insulation)
  • Moisture: Water infiltration in an outlet or junction box
  • Damaged cable: Perforated insulation (construction work, rodents...)
  • Oversensitive RCD: 30mA unsuitable for the installation

Our intervention:

  • Systematic testing of each circuit
  • Insulation resistance measurement
  • Precise fault location
  • Repair or replacement of the faulty component

Never disable the RCD: it protects against electrocution.

For your safety, we advise against any intervention on the electrical installation if you do not have the required skills.

You can do:

  • Change a light bulb

  • Replacing an outlet or switch
  • Repairing a damaged cable
  • Working in the electrical panel
  • Any work on the fixed installation

Risks: electrocution, fire, installation damage, insurance invalidation.

Selectivity limits the impact of a fault on the entire installation:

  • Smart distribution: Circuits distributed across multiple RCDs
  • Adapted sizing: Circuit breakers sized according to loads
  • Discrimination: Only the faulty circuit's protection trips
  • Sensitive circuits isolated: Freezer, alarm, server on dedicated protections

Result: fewer nuisance trips, better service continuity.

Earthing is crucial for personal safety. We check:

  • Conductor continuity: Verification of all protective connections
  • Earth electrode resistance: Measurement with earth tester (< 30Ω required)
  • Equipotential bonding: Connection of metal masses (water, gas, heating)
  • Trip tests: Verification that RCDs respond correctly

The choice depends on required power and equipment:

  • Single-phase (230V): Sufficient up to 9-10 kVA, standard homes
  • Three-phase (400V): Recommended beyond 10 kVA or for powerful equipment

Three-phase recommended if:

  • 11kW or 22kW EV charger
  • Powerful heat pump
  • Workshop with machinery
  • Large area to heat/cool

Three-phase offers several technical advantages:

  • Higher available power: Up to 3× the capacity of single-phase
  • Better load distribution: Balanced distribution across 3 phases
  • Smaller cable sizes: Less current per phase for same power
  • Professional equipment compatibility: Machinery, powerful EV chargers
  • Fewer trips: Distributed load, better absorbed power peaks

Good balancing is essential in three-phase:

  • Strategic distribution: Circuit distribution across the 3 phases
  • Load analysis: Identification of major consumers (oven, dryer, heat pump...)
  • Smart balancing: Distribution to avoid phase overload
  • Measurement and optimization: Current monitoring per phase during commissioning

Goal: network stability, no nuisance trips, equipment longevity.

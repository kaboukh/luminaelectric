RGIE compliance cost in Belgium: what to budget in 2026

Electrical compliance is mandatory for every property sale in Belgium. But what does it actually cost? Here are the real figures, straight to the point.

Home › Blog › RGIE compliance cost

What does RGIE compliance include?

Before discussing prices, it's important to understand what a full compliance package covers. A serious electrician must provide:

  • A complete diagnosis of the existing installation
  • Upgrades to the electrical panel (30mA RCDs, correctly rated circuit breakers)
  • Earth bonding where absent or defective
  • Single-line diagram and position diagram
  • Assistance through inspection by an approved body (Inspect Belgium, KEMA, etc.)
  • The compliance certificate

Most common non-conformities in Belgium

Based on our field experience, here are the main sources of compliance work:

  1. No 30mA RCD (residual current device) — mandatory on all circuits since 1981
  2. Insufficient earthing — especially in houses built before 1960
  3. Outdated panel (fuse wire or ceramic fuses) — full replacement required
  4. Missing diagrams — the inspection body always requires them
  5. Overloaded circuits — too many appliances on a single 16A circuit

How to reduce the cost?

  • Have the diagnosis done before signing a purchase agreement — avoid time pressure
  • Choose an all-in package (materials + labour + diagrams + inspection) — often cheaper than managing each part separately
  • Combine compliance with other electrical work (EV charger, new panel)
